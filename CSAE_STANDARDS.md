# Cuemate — Standards Against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

**Last updated: 2025**

## 1. Our Commitment

Cuemate ("we," "our," or "us") has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). We are committed to preventing, detecting, and removing such content and conduct on our platform and to cooperating with law enforcement and relevant authorities to protect children.

We publish these standards so that users, partners, and the public understand how we address CSAE in line with applicable laws and industry expectations.

---

## 2. Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited on Cuemate:

- **Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)** — Any content that depicts or promotes the sexual abuse or exploitation of minors.
- **Child sexual exploitation (CSE)** — Any conduct that facilitates or encourages the sexual abuse or exploitation of minors, including grooming, solicitation, or trafficking.
- **Content that sexualizes minors** — Imagery, text, or other material that sexualizes or objectifies anyone under the age of 18.
- **Endangering minors** — Any behavior that puts minors at risk of sexual abuse, exploitation, or harm.

We do not allow such content or conduct in any form, including in profiles, photos, messages, or other user-generated content.

---

## 3. Age Requirements and Account Safety

- **Minimum age.** Users must meet the minimum age required to use our Service in their jurisdiction (typically 18 or the age of majority where they live). We do not knowingly allow minors to create accounts where the Service is intended for adults.
- **Age verification.** We may use the information provided at sign-up (including date of birth) and other signals to enforce age requirements and to detect or prevent underage use where the Service is restricted to adults.
- **Account removal.** We will remove accounts that we reasonably believe are used by minors in violation of our age policy or that are linked to CSAE.

---

## 4. Prevention and Detection

We take technical and operational steps to reduce the risk of CSAE on our platform, which may include:

- **Content and behavior policies** that clearly prohibit CSAE and are enforced through our Terms of Service and these standards.
- **User reporting** so that anyone can report content or behavior that may involve CSAE (see Section 5).
- **Review and moderation** of reported content and, where applicable, proactive review of content or accounts that present a higher risk.
- **Use of industry tools and databases** where we integrate or participate in industry efforts to detect known CSAM (e.g. hash-matching systems) in accordance with applicable law and our privacy policy.
- **Training and awareness** for personnel and contractors who may handle safety or moderation matters.

We continue to evaluate and improve our prevention and detection measures.

---

## 5. Reporting CSAE

If you see content or behavior on Cuemate that you believe involves child sexual abuse or exploitation, please report it immediately.

- **In the app:** Use the reporting options available in the app (e.g. report user, report content) and select the option that best describes the issue. We treat CSAE reports as highest priority.
- **By email:** You may contact us at [insert your safety/legal contact email] with "CSAE Report" in the subject line. Include any details that can help us locate the content or account (e.g. username, link, description) without sharing the actual abusive content.
- **To authorities:** You may also report directly to your local law enforcement or to a hotline such as the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline (United States): https://report.cybertip.org/

Do not share CSAM or CSE content with others. Forwarding or distributing such material may be illegal and can re-victimize children.

---

## 6. Our Response to CSAE

When we identify or receive a report of content or conduct that appears to involve CSAE:

- We **remove** the content and take action against the account(s) involved, which may include permanent suspension and referral to law enforcement.
- We **preserve** relevant information in accordance with our legal obligations and internal procedures so that it can be provided to law enforcement or other authorities when required.
- We **report** to the appropriate authorities where required by law (e.g. to NCMEC in the United States under 18 U.S.C. § 2258A, or to equivalent bodies in other jurisdictions).
- We do not **warn** users before removing content or accounts in CSAE cases when doing so could endanger a child or compromise an investigation.

---

## 7. Cooperation with Law Enforcement and Authorities

We cooperate with law enforcement and other competent authorities in the fight against child sexual abuse and exploitation. This may include:

- Responding to lawful requests for user or content information where legally required and in line with our privacy policy and applicable law.
- Providing reports and data to designated authorities (e.g. NCMEC, national hotlines) as required by law.
- Preserving and disclosing information when necessary to protect children or support criminal investigations.

We follow applicable law and our legal processes when responding to such requests.

---
